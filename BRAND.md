# Watchlist — Brand voice

A one-page reference for design + copy decisions. Quotable in
claude.ai design sessions ("here's the brand"). Read this BEFORE
making visual choices, not as an afterthought.

For working conventions, see [CLAUDE.md](CLAUDE.md). For roadmap,
see [ROADMAP.md](ROADMAP.md). For the design tokens this voice
plays out in, see [DESIGN_SYSTEM.md](DESIGN_SYSTEM.md).

## What Watchlist is

A personal vintage-watch tool that reads as a publication. It
aggregates inventory from independent dealers and auction houses
into one feed, lets the user save what they like, and slowly
becomes a thinking-tool for their collecting — wishlists,
watchbox, reflections, hypothetical collections, eventually
reference research and learning. Public site is a secondary
benefit; the user's accumulating taste data is the moat.

It is **not** a marketplace, a deal-hunter, a price-tracker, or a
social network. It does not broker transactions, hide dealer
identities, or compete with Watchcharts on price-per-reference.
Every card links straight to the dealer's own page; we add value
by aggregating + filing + reflecting, not by intercepting.

## Who it's for

The serious vintage-watch collector who already knows their stuff.
Reads Hodinkee. Has opinions about Speedmaster pre-Moon refs.
Tracks the same five dealers manually before this site existed.
Doesn't need education on what a tropical dial is. Wants to *not
miss* what just dropped, *file* what they've found, and eventually
*understand* what they own better than the dealer does.

## Voice attributes

- **Restrained.** No exclamation marks, no "🔥 hot drop", no
  banners shouting "NEW!". The watch is the artifact; the chrome
  steps back.
- **Editorially literate.** Sentence-case body, occasional small-
  caps eyebrows, italic for tag-lines. The register is closer to
  *Monocle* or *Hodinkee* than to *Chrono24* or *eBay*.
- **Quietly confident.** "Recently added" not "🆕 LATEST DROPS".
  "View catalog" not "View Catalog →→". The user knows what to
  do; we don't shout instructions.
- **Personal-tool-first.** "Your hearted watches", not "Your
  saves" or "Your favourites". "Mark as sold", not "List for
  sale". The voice talks to a collector with a private
  collection, not a power-user dashboard.
- **No emoji as decoration.** Emoji appear only where they carry
  semantic weight (👍 / ❌ / ❤️ in reactions, 📋 in the To-review
  bucket, 🎉 on screening completion). Never as ornament.

## What it sounds like (vs doesn't)

| Watchlist says | Watchlist doesn't say |
|---|---|
| "Recently added" | "🚀 Hot drops this week" |
| "Closing tomorrow" | "Don't miss out — ends in 1 day!" |
| "No bids yet" | "Be the first to bid!" |
| "Mark as sold" | "List your watch for sale" |
| "View catalog" | "Click here to browse" |
| "Sold today" | "Just sold ✅" |
| "Your reactions saved" | "Got it — your vote counts!" |
| "Pulling the latest listings…" | "Loading…" |

## Visual register direction

Editorial-restrained. The reference points are *Hodinkee*,
*Fratello*, *Monocle*, *Aesop*, *Linear* (for layout discipline).
Not Chrono24, not Bezel, not Watchbox. Specifically:

- **Type**: a real type ramp with one editorial heading face
  (system stack works too, but with care). Body in system sans.
  Tabular numerals for prices + counts.
- **Colour**: restrained palette. One brand colour, neutral
  greys, subtle accent for positive (green) and danger (red).
  No gradients, no shadows beyond hairlines.
- **Spacing**: generous, not cramped. The page breathes. White
  space is the design.
- **Lines + dividers**: hairline rules carry hierarchy where
  weight changes would shout. 0.5px borders, never 2px.
- **Cards**: the watch image is the hero. Chrome (heart, ⋯, hide
  ×) sits as low-contrast overlays that fade visually until tap.
- **Motion**: subtle and quick (~200ms ease-out), used to confirm
  state changes — not to entertain.

## Words to use, words to avoid

**Use**: feed, listings, lots, dealers, auction houses, references,
saved, hearted, hidden, watchbox, wishlist, screen (verb, "screen
the catalog"), pick, pass.

**Avoid**: drops, deals, bargains, hot, trending, viral, exclusive,
limited time, FOMO copy, "act now", "don't miss", "for you" (as in
algorithmic feed framing), engagement, growth, scale, monetise,
power user, hack, pro tips, "shop now".

## What "good" looks like in five years

Someone reads about Watchlist on r/watches and the comment is
"the only watch tool that respects collectors enough to not look
like a marketplace." The interface is invisible. The references
encyclopedia rivals what dealers themselves write. The user's
five-year watchbox journal reads back to them like a diary they
forgot they'd kept. Mark has had two offers from established
publications to acquire and turned both down because the tool is
how he himself collects now.

That's the lane. Every visual decision points there.
